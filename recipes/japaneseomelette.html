Japanese Omelette

Description

A Japanese omelette is denser than the Western type and is typically cut into slices. These can then be either eaten traight away, chilled for up to three days, or wrapped and frozen.

When sugar is added, it is usually used to top or fill sushi; whereas the sugar-free version is used in hot food. Obviously children prefer the version with sugar!

Japanese Omelette Without Sugar

Ingredients:

  • 11/2 tsp cornflour
  • 6 free-range eggs
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil (optional)

Steps:

  1. Dissolve the cornflour in 7 tbsp of water in a large bowl. Beat in the eggs with the salt.
  2. Add the sesame oil to a frying pan over a medium heat. (If you use a non-stick pan you won't need the oil).
  3. Add one laddle's worth of egg mixture to the pan, tilting it until the base is competely covered. Cook until the egg mixtureis just about to set.
  4. Fold the egg over on itself three times in 6cm folds. keep the omelette in the pan, and laddle more egg into the newly made space, lifting the first omelette with a spatula to allow the raw egg to flow underneath.
  5. Once the new egg mixture is about to set, fold three times again, encasing the first omelette, creating a layered effect. Repeat until the egg mixture has been used up. Serve, or divide into six, wrap and freeze separately.

Serves Six: 516 kcal in total, 86 kcal per serving

Japanese Omelette With Sugar

Make as above, but add to the egg mixture:

  • 1/2 tsp bonito flakes, dissolved in a little water
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 11/2 tbsp caster sugar

Serves Six: 872 kcal in total, 145 kcal per serving
